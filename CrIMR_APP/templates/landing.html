What is Delayed Sleep Phase Disorder (DSPD)?

Delayed Sleep Phase Disorder (DSPD) is one of the most common genetically-caused sleep disorder where individuals inherit a late sleep/wake cycle, sleeping at least 1-2 hours later than recommended. Being presentin in 15% of adolescents and adults, its genetic backing remains the cause for 40-50% cases. Yet current methods of diagnosis, such as DLMO's and PSG's, fail to acknolwedge the genetic aspect, restricting personalized diagnosis and treatment.
